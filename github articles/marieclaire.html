Is unemployment contagious at 50 years old? - Testimony

For eight years, Margaux Gilquin has alternated between unemployment and insecure jobs. Today, she is still looking for a regular job. She relates the tale of this uphill battle in a book entitled “The Last Salary”. In it, she reveals with irony and anger her pains and constant job research.

Olivia Bugault: Why did you talk about your misfortune in a book?

Margaux Gilquin: I was so angry because I fought hard after my layoff in 2008 to find a new position. Today, I live with 487 euros a month. I wanted people to know that no one wants to be “assisted”; no one wants to sell their furniture as I did. I turn my anger into courage with this book. I don’t lose time anymore: I move forward.


“We can do it if we want to”: is this maxim true when, as you, we faced seven years of professional hurdles?

No, it is not. To wish is not enough. Recruiters are not allowed or don’t want to hire seniors. Recently, an employer was searching for commercials and told me: “I cannot hire you because, after fifty years, your capacity to integrate information has decreased”. I really heard it!

Reading your book, it seems that job centres are unable to efficiently help you.

Since I moved near Bordeaux, my job advisers really take care of me. But in Paris it was awful. You have to register and fill a form to know if you are eligible for allowances. Then, there is no support, no one to explain you the methodology, the complexity of the process. I didn’t know how to look for a job. At 50 years old, we don’t know how to use employment research website. I lost time because of their incompetence, lack of reactivity and information provided.

Is unemployment contagious?

Unemployment can happen to everyone so it’s frightening. I lost many friends during my unemployment period and those I have today often are in the same situation than me. At the beginning, it was frustrating but my priority was to find a work. It was hard enough to stick on my tight budget so even if friendship is important, I tried to not get irritated by this problem.

Are there only two options for newcomers on the labour market: be a shark or get crushed?

Today, people learn how to survive in the labour market. The young recruiters I met have cut their teeth on job seekers in a way they probably learned in business schools. There is no humanity during an interview. I got interviews with young women who told me: “Listen grandma, you are still an assistant at 50 years old whereas me, I am a project manager”. It’s craven but can we be angry with victims of the system? I am not mad at them.

How do you manage to live with 487 euros per month?

You have to account for your spending every day. I have shoes that I need to repair but I don’t have the money for it. I don’t smoke anymore. And I don’t hang out with my friends neither. But I have been raised poorly so it’s not the problem. The real difference is that I see people enjoying their life all around me while I am struggling for a job, for my survival. It hurts deeply.

However, your book is full of self-mockery and humour…

It’s crucial otherwise you just hang yourself! I said it to the Minister of Labour, Myriam El Khomri. She invited me because she liked my book. We spent one and a half hour together. But when she asked me how I envisioned my future, I answered “at the end of a rope”!
